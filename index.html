Article One

Congress shall be given the power, by the Constitution, to make laws. Congress will be comprised of two houses: The Senate and the House of Representatives. House of Reps Term for a representative is two years. Criteria for becoming a representative: Must be at least 25 Must have lived in the United States for at least seven years Must be an inhabitant of the state for which they will represent. Taxation and representation=dependant on population of state ⅗’s of the slaves counted for population. 14th amendment repealed this. 1 representative for every 30 people Senate Term for a senate member is six years Must be at least 30 years Must have lived in the United States for nine years Must be an inhabitant of the state for which he/she chooses Vice President is in charge of the senators. The Vice doesn’t count as a vote unless there is a tie. (House of Reps+Senate=first 6 sections) What protections does Congress have? The members of Congress cannot be arrested when in a current session or going to their house, for any reason. The Members cannot be questioned either about a speech or debate. Laws Law-making begins at the House of Representatives. Then it goes to the Senate to get approved. When approved by both houses, it goes to the President. When the President signs, it becomes a law. In the case that the President doesn’t, the bill goes to Congress. Congress can overrule the President’s decision by getting two-thirds of the votes from both houses on the bill. In the case that the bill is not returned by the President to Congress, the bill automatically becomes law. If Congress disbanded the bill before the President could return the bill, it does not become law. ELASTIC CLAUSE: Congress has the power to pass all laws necessary and proper for carrying out enumerated powers. What can Congress do? Congress can: Tax its citizens Can pay loans or debt if they wanted to Common defense and welfare for citizens Get loans Call war measures if needed to Naturalization policies/legislation Manage trade relations with foreign countries Form an army Can force small state militia to enforce laws Take in new states admitted to the U.S.

Article Two

Creates the job of President, called the Executive. Responsible for enforcing the laws. Section 1 Establishes the office of the President and the Vice-President. Both serve for four years. Presidents are elected by the Electoral College. 35 years old. Must be born in the USA. Their pay cannot change, up or down, as long as he is in office. Section 2 President leads the armed forces. He has a Cabinet to help him, and can pardon criminals. He makes treaties with other nations (2/3 of the Senate have to approve of the treaty). Picks many of the judges and other members of the government. Section 3 President must give a yearly speech to the nation. Give suggestions to Congress. Meet with Ambassadors and other heads of state from other nations. Ensure the laws of the United States are carried out. Section 4 A. Explains how to kick the president from office, called impeachment.

Article Three

Article 3 deals with the judicial branch. The Judicial branch are the law and court cases. This includes supreme court cases and other courts created by congress. Section 1 The federal court system has to have one supreme court Includes chief justice and 8 other justices Congress can create and remove lower courts, there doesn’t have to be any if they don’t want them A judge can be in office until they die, are convicted, or impeached A judge's pay can be increased, but it cannot be decreased Section 2 Who gets power The supreme court can decide what cases they want to see The supreme court has the right to see any case if they would like to A defendant doesn’t have to get a jury if he or she does not want one Section 3 Treason When you launch any type of attack against the United States and you are a citizen of the U.S. AT least two witnesses to the act Or person can confess to treason
