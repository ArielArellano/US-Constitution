Article One

Congress, according to the elastic clause, has all the powers to make laws that it deems appropriate. Congress is comprised of two houses: The house of Representatives and the Senate. The two houses work together to make and pass legislation. Both positions require different sets of criteria. In order to make a law, both houses must agree upon it. Then, it reaches the president for which he has to agree on it. If he signs it, it becomes a law. If it is not signed, it goes back to congress, for which it has to receive ⅔’s of the votes in each house to then become a law. Congress has certain protections and powers that it can decide to use. It can tax, pay loans, get loans, call war, form army, admit states, etc. Congress also has the protections to not be arrested if they are in a meeting, for whichever reason.

Article Two

Creates the job of President, called the Executive. Responsible for enforcing the laws.

  • Section 1: Establishes the office of the President and the Vice-President. Both serve for four years. Presidents are elected by the Electoral College. 35 years old. Must be born in the USA. Their pay cannot change, up or down, as long as he is in office.
  • Section 2: President leads the armed forces. He has a Cabinet to help him, and can pardon criminals. He makes treaties with other nations (2/3 of the Senate have to approve of the treaty). Picks many of the judges and other members of the government.
  • Section 3: President must give a yearly speech to the nation. Give suggestions to Congress. Meet with Ambassadors and other heads of state from other nations. Ensure the laws of the United States are carried out.
  • Section 4: Explains how to kick the president from office, called impeachment.

    • Article Three

    Deals with the judicial branch. The Judicial branch are the law and court cases. This includes supreme court cases and other courts created by congress.

  • Section 1: The federal court system has to have one supreme court.
    • Includes chief justice and 8 other justices. Congress can create and remove lower courts, there doesn’t have to be any if they don’t want them. A judge can be in office until they die, are convicted, or impeached. A judge's pay can be increased, but it cannot be decreased.
  • Section 2: Who gets power:
    • The supreme court can decide what cases they want to see. The supreme court has the right to see any case if they would like to. A defendant doesn’t have to get a jury if he or she does not want one.
  • Section 3: Treason
    • When you launch any type of attack against the United States and you are a citizen of the U.S.. AT least two witnesses to the act. Or person can confess to treason.

    Article Five

    Making Amendments or Changes

  • 1.Proposal
      • a. 2/3 of Congress (Senate and House) can vote for proposing a change.
        b. 2/3 of State Legislatures can vote for a convention to propose a change.
          2.Ratification
        ¾ of States must vote to ratify amendments(either state legislatures or special state convention).

  • National government is supreme in conflicts over individual states.
  • State law cannot contradict national law.
